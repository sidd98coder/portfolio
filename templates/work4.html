004 : Watermarks to pDF

Introduction

A watermark is a message (usually a logo, stamp, or signature) superimposed onto an image or any document, with a great deal of transparency. Watermarks are often used by photo banks and by people or institutions that care about the use of their digital works to protect their interests. Watermarks are extremely useful in a variety of ways. Since the advent of this technique to the present day, the most common use has been to protect the copyright of any type of document. Watermarks discourage the illegal use of creative material without the permission of its creator. That’s why, as you’ve probably noticed, it’s not always easy to find free and legal photos to use in your posts.

How it works ?

This program will helps users to watermark each page of a PDF file. It takes two PDFs as input in which one contain watermarked symbol Pdf and the other one is Pdf to be watermarked.
